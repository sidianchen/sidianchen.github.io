About me

I am a PostDoc researcher working with Prof. Hamdi Tchelepi at the Department of Energy Science & Engineering at Stanford University. I completed my Ph.D. degree under the supervision of Dr. Bo Guo at the University of Arizona. My research focuses on pore-scale modeling of multiphase fluid flow, multispecies transport, and thermodynamic phase change behaviors in geological and manufactured porous materials. My research applications include the shale gas/oil production, geological hydrogen storage, vadose zone emerging PFAS contamination, vadose zone evaporation processes, and ion transport in next-generation rechargeable batteries. The overarching goal is to leverage fundamental porous media physics to address global challenges in energy transition and environmental sustainability.
